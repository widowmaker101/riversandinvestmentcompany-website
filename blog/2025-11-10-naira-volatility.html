Understanding the 2025 Naira Landscape: Key Statistics

The naira's journey in 2025 has been marked by Central Bank interventions and policy shifts. From January to November, the official rate hovered between ₦1,421 and ₦1,437 per USD, with parallel market volatility pushing it to ₦1,440–₦1,470. [](grok_render_citation_card_json={"cardIds":["30753b"]}) Nairalytics projects a stable range for the year: +5% appreciation to -10% depreciation, a vast improvement from 2024's 40% nosedive. [](grok_render_citation_card_json={"cardIds":["a92168"]}) Yet, risks persist—global Fed rate cuts (67% chance of 25bps in December 2025) could strengthen the USD, exacerbating local pressures.   • Depreciation Impact: A 1% naira drop erodes portfolio value by 0.7–1.2% for unhedged investors (CBN data, Q3 2025).

  • Market Sentiment: Forex remittances hit $600M monthly by mid-2025, but speculative attacks caused 1–1.4% weekly swings (NFEM, November 3–9). [](grok_render_citation_card_json={"cardIds":["77564b"]})
