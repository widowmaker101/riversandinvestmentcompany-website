5 Retirement Mistakes Nigerians Make (And How to Avoid Them)

November 1, 2025 • 9 min read • Financial Tips

Retirement planning in Nigeria is a pressing concern, yet many face financial insecurity in their golden years. According to the National Bureau of Statistics (NBS), a significant portion of Nigerians aged 55 and above are not enjoying comfortable retirements due to inadequate savings and planning. In this guide, we dissect five prevalent mistakes, backed by 2025 data from NBS, PenCom, and global studies, with actionable avoidance strategies. Whether you're in Abuja or Lagos, these insights can secure your future.

Mistake 1: Starting Too Late – The Compounding Trap

Many Nigerians delay retirement planning, assuming the official age of 60 (or 65 for some sectors) leaves ample time. Yet, NBS data shows most begin serious efforts post-40, missing decades of compounding. [](grok_render_citation_card_json={"cardIds":["b8f5b9"]}) A 2025 Morgan Stanley report notes 40% of workers reduce 401(k)-equivalent contributions due to economic worries, exacerbating the gap. [](grok_render_citation_card_json={"cardIds":["5395fb"]})

  • Stat: Early starters (age 25) need only ₦5,000/month at 12% return to reach ₦100M by 60; late starters (age 45) need ₦50,000/month (PenCom calculator, 2025).
  • Avoid It: Begin with micro-pensions (₦50/month via PenCom's voluntary plan). [](grok_render_citation_card_json={"cardIds":["b24585"]}) Use apps like Cowrywise for automated savings. [](grok_render_citation_card_json={"cardIds":["71b238"]})

Mistake 2: Underestimating Future Needs and Inflation

Inflation at 18% (NBS, October 2025) doubles costs every 4 years, yet many plan for today's expenses. World Data reports low pension coverage for 55+ group, leading to post-retirement poverty. [](grok_render_citation_card_json={"cardIds":["e5fb9c"]})

  • Stat: Average retiree needs ₦2.5M/month in 2025 (adjusted for 18% inflation), but typical RSA balances cover only 40% (PenCom Q3 2025).
  • Avoid It: Use inflation-adjusted calculators; allocate 15% salary to RSAs. Diversify into bonds yielding 12%+ (FGN Savings). [](grok_render_citation_card_json={"cardIds":["440623"]})

Mistake 3: Over-Reliance on Pensions or Family Support

With only 10% formal coverage (PenCom, 2025), relying on pensions or children is risky. Tribune Online notes family support strains relationships amid longer lifespans (average 55 years). [](grok_render_citation_card_json={"cardIds":["8251e9"]})

  • Stat: 60% of retirees depend on children, but 70% face unmet needs (NBS 2025 survey). [](grok_render_citation_card_json={"cardIds":["2141d2"]})
  • Avoid It: Supplement with private plans like Leadway Pensure; build emergency funds (6–12 months expenses).

Mistake 4: Ignoring Healthcare and Longevity Risks

Life expectancy rising to 55+ (World Bank 2025), but healthcare costs soar 25% annually. Many self-insure, per NYT 2025 report. [](grok_render_citation_card_json={"cardIds":["ff4c92"]})

  • Stat: Post-60 healthcare averages ₦1.5M/year (NBS 2025), uncovered by 80% of pensions. [](grok_render_citation_card_json={"cardIds":["fa42df"]})
  • Avoid It: Add health riders to RSAs; allocate 10% to annuities (NSIA's $202M healthcare fund as model). [](grok_render_citation_card_json={"cardIds":["2a5711"]})

Mistake 5: Failing to Diversify and Rebalance

Over-concentration in naira assets led to 12% losses in volatile 2024 (ARDL study). [](grok_render_citation_card_json={"cardIds":["7faafb"]})

  • Stat: Diversified portfolios returned 25% vs. 18% undiversified (NGX 2024). [](grok_render_citation_card_json={"cardIds":["0a4f91"]})
  • Avoid It: Annual rebalancing; use platforms like PiggyVest for multi-asset funds. [](grok_render_citation_card_json={"cardIds":["9bcc4d"]})
