5 Retirement Mistakes Nigerians Make (And How to Avoid Them)

November 1, 2025 • 9 min read • Financial Tips

According to the National Bureau of Statistics, 70% of Nigerians aged 55+ do not have adequate retirement savings. Here are the 5 biggest mistakes — and how to fix them.

1. Starting Too Late

Begin at 25: ₦5,000/month at 12% return = ₦100M by 60.
Start at 45: You need ₦50,000/month.

2. Underestimating Inflation

At 18% inflation, costs double every 4 years. Plan for ₦2.5M/month in today’s terms.

3. Relying Only on Pension or Family

Only 10% have formal pension coverage. Build private plans.

4. Ignoring Healthcare Costs

Post-60 healthcare: ₦1.5M/year. Add health riders to your RSA.

5. Failing to Diversify

Diversified portfolios returned 25% vs 18% undiversified (NGX 2024).
